# 📊 Colgate-Palmolive (India) Ltd – 3 Statement Financial Model (FY2023–FY2030)

**Author:** Ankit Kuldeep  

---

## 🔹 Project Overview

This project presents a fully integrated 3-statement financial model of Colgate-Palmolive (India) Ltd built using historical financial data from FY2023–FY2025 and forecasted through FY2030.

The objective of this model is to demonstrate practical financial modelling capability through structured linkage of:

- Income Statement  
- Balance Sheet  
- Cash Flow Statement  

The model incorporates revenue forecasting, operating cost projections, working capital modelling, capital expenditure planning, depreciation scheduling, dividend payout assumptions, and cash reconciliation.

The cash flow statement is prepared using the **Indirect Method**, with operating cash derived from Profit Before Tax, non-cash adjustments, and changes in working capital.

The model is fully linked, meaning changes in assumptions dynamically flow through all financial statements while maintaining balance sheet integrity.

---

# 🔹 Income Statement Assumptions

### Revenue
Revenue from operations is projected based on historical growth trends and forward-looking stability assumptions.

### Operating Expenses
Key cost components including:
- Cost of materials consumed  
- Purchase of stock-in-trade  
- Employee benefits expense  
- Other operating expenses  

are projected as a percentage of revenue using historical averages to maintain realistic operating leverage.

### EBITDA & EBIT
EBITDA is derived after operating expenses.  
Depreciation is linked directly to the PPE schedule to ensure consistency between capital expenditure and expense recognition.

EBIT is calculated after deducting depreciation from EBITDA.

### Finance Cost
Finance costs are projected based on the company’s minimal leverage profile and stable liability structure.

### Taxation
Income tax expense is forecast using the historical effective tax rate.

### Net Profit
Profit After Tax (PAT) flows into retained earnings and drives dividend payout projections.

# 🔹 Balance Sheet Assumptions

## Assets

### Deferred Tax Asset
Deferred tax assets are projected conservatively following stabilization observed in recent years.

### Other Non-Current Assets
Projected at a conservative 5% annual growth rate reflecting stable capital advances and long-term deposits.

### Current Tax Assets
Projected in line with tax expense growth patterns.

### Loans
Loans under financial assets represent advances and deposits given. As these have remained stable historically, they are projected at constant levels.

### Other Financial Assets
Projected at 5% annual growth reflecting operational scale expansion.

### Other Current Assets
Projected in line with revenue growth (8%) as they represent operational prepayments and statutory receivables.

### Bank Balances
Bank balances are treated as the balancing figure in forecast years to ensure total assets equal total equity and liabilities. These represent short-term deposits and surplus liquidity.

---

## Liabilities

### Other Current Liabilities
Projected in line with revenue growth due to their operational nature.

### Current Tax Liabilities
Projected in line with tax expense growth.

### Provisions
Projected at 6% annually reflecting operational and employee-related accruals.

### Lease Liabilities (Current & Non-Current)
Projected assuming gradual amortisation of existing lease contracts and stable operating footprint.

### Other Financial Liabilities
Projected at modest 4–5% annual growth reflecting stable financial accruals.

### Other Non-Current Liabilities
Assumed to decline gradually at 5% annually in line with historical trend.

# 🔹 PPE, Depreciation & Capex

Depreciation is projected using weighted average useful lives disclosed in accounting policies, resulting in an effective depreciation rate of approximately 17%.

Historical depreciation averaged ~19.5% of gross PPE; however, considering disclosed useful lives (Buildings: 20–40 years, Plant & Equipment: 7–15 years, Furniture & Equipment: 5 years), a normalized 17% rate is applied in forecast years.

Capital Work-in-Progress (CWIP) is projected assuming 15% of annual capex remains under construction at year-end, reflecting typical 1–2 year completion cycles.

---

# 🔹 Equity & Dividend Policy

The company follows a high dividend payout policy historically (85%–110%).  
A dividend payout ratio of 85% has been assumed for forecast years to reflect its mature and cash-generative profile.

Retained earnings are rolled forward accordingly within the equity schedule.
